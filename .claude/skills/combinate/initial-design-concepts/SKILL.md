# Skill: Initial Design Concepts Email

Erin sends this email at the start of the design phase for almost all projects. It hands over two design concepts (style guide + homepage) and guides the client on how to review and give feedback in Figma.

## When to Use

- "Send the design concepts email"
- "Draft the design handover email"
- "Share the designs with the client"
- Two design options are ready and need to be sent to a client for review

## What You Need Before Starting

- Client email addresses
- Option 1: style guide link + Figma **prototype** link
- Option 2: style guide link + Figma **prototype** link
- A visual guide showing how Figma comments work (screenshot or GIF — Erin usually has one ready at a short URL like cbo.d.pr)
- Any placeholder content to flag (e.g. stats, imagery)

## Figma Links

Always use **prototype links** (`figma.com/proto/`) for client-facing emails, not design view links (`figma.com/design/`). Prototype links open in a clean viewer with scaling applied — much better for clients.

Example format:
```
https://www.figma.com/proto/FILE_KEY/File-Name?node-id=NODE_ID&scaling=scale-down&content-scaling=fixed&page-id=PAGE_ID&starting-point-node-id=NODE_ID
```

## Email Structure

**Subject:** [Client Name] Website — Two Design Concepts for Your Review

**To:** Client contacts
**CC:** shane@combinate.me

---

**Opening paragraph**
We've put together two initial design concepts for the [CLIENT] website. Each comes with a style guide, defining the visual language, colours and typography, and a full homepage mockup. We'd love your feedback to better understand your preferences and the visual direction you envision for [CLIENT].

---

**Option 1 — [Short, plain name e.g. "Clean and polished"]**

Style Guide: [link]
Homepage: [Figma prototype link]

2–3 sentences describing the direction in plain language. Note any placeholder content (stats, imagery, copy) and ask for real numbers/assets where needed. E.g. "We've added some placeholder figures here — it would be great to get your actual numbers."

---

**Option 2 — [Short, plain name e.g. "Bold and fun"]**

Style Guide: [link]
Homepage: [Figma prototype link]

2–3 sentences describing the direction. Keep the language accessible — avoid design jargon like "editorial" or "benefit-led."

---

**Viewing the designs and leaving feedback**

Both homepages are in Figma. It may take a minute or two to load all of the imagery and videos — don't worry, the developed site will be faster.

We've added comments directly onto the designs to explain our thinking in context, flag a few questions, and note where animation will be added during development (as we can't include it in the design). Look for the small speech bubble icons as you scroll through, which you can click on to see and reply to the comments: [link to visual guide]

Both designs include a similar content structure, but are vastly different in look and feel. We are really keen to get your feedback, good and bad, to understand your likes, dislikes and preferences — it is all helpful for us to move forward and build something you're proud of.

You can leave your feedback directly in the homepage design files. To do this:
1. Enter "comment mode" by clicking the speech bubble in the top menu
2. Click the area you want to comment on
3. Type your note and press **Enter** to post it

If you'd prefer to talk through both options together, we're more than happy to jump on a call — just let me know a time that suits you.

I look forward to hearing your thoughts!

[Erin's signature]

---

## Option Naming Conventions

Use short, plain, client-friendly names. Examples:
- "Clean and polished" / "Bold and fun"
- "Light and minimal" / "Rich and textured"
- "Classic and structured" / "Modern and editorial"

Avoid design-industry language like "benefit-led", "editorial", "typographic-led" — clients don't think in those terms.
